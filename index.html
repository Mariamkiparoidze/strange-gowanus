The Stranger Things of Gowanus

By Mariam Kiparoidze

October 7, 2022

Stretched across 1,8 miles in Brooklyn, New York surrounded by more calm neighborhoods like Park Slope and Cobble Hill, is the infamous Gowanus Canal. The Gowanus Canal was built in the 19th century and was a big part of industrial transportation routes. Over the centuries though, the industrial waste poured into the water along with sewage and it did not flow out. "Water from the Upper New York Bay flowed in, but then just… stayed there,” Dan Nosowitz wrote in Popular Science.

So the factories and other manufacturing sites around, coupled with sewage flow created a waterway that’s become notorious for being one of the most polluted and contaminated in the United States. In 2010 the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) declared the Gowanus Canal a Superfund site. In 2020 EPA approved a $1.5 billion cleaning operation and in spite of environmental concerns the site has seen interest from developers.

Over the centuries, the foul odor and contamination gained the canal many descriptions and nicknames such as “Perfume Creek,” for example.

Some scientists believe there are unique organisms living there even though it’s full of toxic chemicals. It’s an unstudied site but there have been many interesting and fascinating events and findings in and around the Gowanus that we’ve heard. Here are three from recent years.
